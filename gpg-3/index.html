About the TCS community on Twitter:

How would you characterize the TCS community on twitter (eg. size, kinds of people, attitudes, dynamics)?


It's pretty positive and supportive. They haven’t really seen arguments. Anything you post is linked to your name forever and academia is very reputation based, so people only wanna show best side. Academics will privately say that a paper is bad or its methodology is silly, and people will complain online about anonymous reviews but not about spcific papers.


There's a diplomatic tone when debunking, and lots of effort goes into preserving this tone. People are wrong all the time, so it's better way to operate to be generous in criticism. Judgements on what’s interesting are central to academia and can be controversial, but people keep negative opinions to themselves more.


Most of the people are between 25-55, usually younger professors, grad students, and postdocs. There are also lurking undergrads, who don't sat much because they both haven't done much and can be intimidated.


There is some correlation between academic success and Twitter success, but it's not that strong. It's more correlated with how much you post fun facts, cool techniques, and explainers.

Why do you decide to like/retweet tweets?


They will like non-controversial things. "No saucy takes".


Are there politics on TCS twitter? Beef? Subsections that elicit strong/any emotional reactions?


There is definitely beef between academics, but it's not usually hashed out on Twitter. More often there are thoughts on practices in the community, advice for writing papers, general wisdom, and paper threads. When target of anger is not person, people will express their anger.
