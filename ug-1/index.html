About work and Twitter:

Has Twitter impacted the way you learn about your field?


Definitely yeah, Twitter is a service where people interact and where people are pretty honest. I can read all these papers but on Twitter all these actual scientists are interacting as people and talking to each other in a very natural way. It's a good way to see who is in this field and who they are as people. The people whose pages I check regularly, they postly post TCS related things but there’s also adjacent things (ex. representation in CS)


What about keeping up to date with new discoveries?


Definitely! If a finding is very influential, someone will tweet about it. There’s the arxiv, and I follow that, but I don’t know enough to understand what’s good and what’s not. Endorsements of work are very useful.


About the TCS community on Twitter:

How would you characterize the TCS community on Twitter (eg. size, kinds of people, attitudes, dynamics)?


The people I know personally are at Harvard, and it seems like a pretty small group who interact and know each other. The conversation seems dominated by faculty.

I’m pretty sure there is, but even if people have beef offline they're pretty civil on Twitter. Academic Twitter feels like a professional forum and people keep things civil. There are external conflicts though, like the thing with Jelani and that Stanford professor.
